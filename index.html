Principal Investigator

Dr. Bikram Paul

Assistant Professor, School of Computing and Electrical Engineering, IIT Mandi

Dr. Bikram Paul is an Assistant Professor at the School of Computing and Electrical Engineering (SCEE), Indian Institute of Technology Mandi. Prior to joining IIT Mandi, he served as an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering at BITS Pilani, Goa Campus.

He completed his post-doctoral research at the Technical University of Munich (TUM), Germany, and earned his Ph.D. from the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. His academic journey also includes a visiting lecturer position at the Tripura Institute of Technology.

Dr. Paul has led significant international collaborations, including a joint project between India and the Czech Republic focused on developing smart contactless fencing systems. His research vision bridges theoretical foundations with practical hardware implementations for next-generation secure computing systems.

Research Areas

Our lab focuses on the critical intersection of hardware design, security, and emerging technologies to build resilient computing systems for the quantum era.

Post-Quantum Cryptography

Designing and implementing cryptographic algorithms and hardware accelerators that remain secure against attacks from both classical and quantum computers, ensuring long-term data security in the post-quantum era.

View Publications

Hardware Security

Developing secure hardware accelerators for System-on-Chip (SoC) designs and implementing countermeasures against physical attacks, side-channel analysis, and hardware Trojans to ensure system integrity.

View Publications

RISC-V Processor Design

Architecting open-source, energy-efficient processor cores based on the RISC-V instruction set architecture, with focus on security extensions, custom accelerators, and verification methodologies.

View Publications

System-on-Chip (SoC) Design

Integrating processing elements, memory subsystems, and specialized accelerators into cohesive SoC architectures for AI-powered autonomous vehicles, IoT edge devices, and high-performance computing applications.

View Publications

Secure ML Architecture

Designing hardware architectures that enable secure and efficient Machine Learning inference and training in cloud and edge environments, protecting model intellectual property and user data privacy.

View Publications

Biomedical IoT Security

Creating secure, low-power architectures for biomedical IoT devices that ensure patient data privacy, system reliability, and regulatory compliance in healthcare applications.

View Publications

Lab Facilities

State-of-the-art equipment and tools supporting our cutting-edge research in hardware security and digital system design.

VLSI Design Lab

Advanced EDA tools including Cadence, Synopsys, and Mentor Graphics for RTL design, synthesis, place-and-route, and verification of complex digital systems.

Hardware Security Lab

Specialized equipment for side-channel analysis, fault injection, and physical attack evaluation including power analyzers, EM probes, and glitch injection setups.

FPGA Prototyping Cluster

High-end FPGA boards (Xilinx Virtex UltraScale+, Intel Stratix 10) for rapid prototyping of processor architectures, cryptographic accelerators, and SoC designs.

AI/ML Hardware Lab

Dedicated workstations with GPU accelerators for developing and evaluating hardware architectures for machine learning inference and training workloads.

IoT Testbed

Comprehensive IoT development environment with sensor nodes, gateway devices, and cloud integration platforms for evaluating security and performance of edge computing systems.
